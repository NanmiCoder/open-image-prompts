# Architecture and interior style cards

Treat architecture as spatial sequencing, material ratios and light behavior. Do not substitute a list of fashionable furniture names for an actual spatial concept.

## Organic modern courtyard cafe

- `id`: `organic-modern-courtyard-cafe`
- `name_en`: Organic Modern Courtyard Cafe
- `name_zh`: 有机现代庭院咖啡空间
- `validation_status`: experimental-corpus-reviewed / generation-pending (single-source cluster)
- `trigger`: Calm cafes, adaptive reuse, garden restaurants, hospitality interiors, indoor-outdoor threshold concepts.
- `not_for`: Plush luxury hotels, maximalist retail, futuristic labs, tiny product close-ups.
- `visual_dna`: A mature tree is the spatial anchor; rough masonry and microcement meet aged timber, blackened metal, terrazzo and cane; overhead slats modulate daylight; circulation dissolves around a courtyard.
- `composition_light_color_material`: Wide eye-level architectural view; foreground threshold frames a deep sequence; one-point or restrained two-point perspective; hard daylight softened through beams and foliage; charcoal, brick, weathered wood, river stone and muted green.
- `boundaries`: Tree and path must affect the plan, not decorate a corner. Keep furniture low and sparse. Use a 50/30/20 material hierarchy: quiet primary surfaces, warm natural accents, precise metal/glass details.
- `template`: `An organic-modern [CAFE / HOSPITALITY SPACE] organized around one mature living tree in an interior courtyard, layered threshold from street to counter to garden, rough microcement and dark brick primary surfaces, aged timber ceiling beams, black terrazzo service counter, cane-and-wood furniture, river-stone tree bed and precise glass partitions, wide eye-level architectural photograph, sunlight filtered through slatted roof and foliage, calm earthy palette, deep spatial clarity, no people posing, no text, no logo.`
- `anti_patterns`: Random potted plants; fake moss on every wall; glossy shopping-mall finishes; furniture showroom symmetry; tree with no root zone; harsh HDR.

## Cinematic amber luxury interior

- `id`: `cinematic-amber-luxury-interior`
- `name_en`: Cinematic Amber Luxury Interior
- `name_zh`: 琥珀电影感奢华室内
- `validation_status`: experimental-corpus-reviewed / generation-pending (single-source cluster)
- `trigger`: Boutique hotel rooms, private cabins, premium lounges, restrained residential luxury and material mood studies.
- `not_for`: Bright Scandinavian catalogues, family playrooms, white-background ecommerce, wide real-estate documentation.
- `visual_dna`: Luxury communicated by light on material rather than object count; amber side light cuts long graphic shadows; polished wood, brass, leather and linen reflect at different strengths; dark negative space creates intimacy.
- `composition_light_color_material`: Close architectural vignette or sparse wide room; low-key exposure; warm hard side light from one believable opening; mahogany, tobacco, brass, navy and warm linen; deep reflections kept below clipping.
- `boundaries`: One dominant light source. Two premium material families plus a textile are enough. Let darkness remain dark. No decorative object pile or chandelier-as-proof-of-luxury.
- `template`: `A cinematic interior photograph of [LUXURY SPACE], composed as a restrained architectural vignette, one warm low-angle side light casting long geometric shadows across [WOOD / STONE / LEATHER], controlled reflections on polished surfaces, accents of aged brass and tactile linen, deep tobacco and navy shadows, sparse furnishings, quiet private atmosphere, realistic low-key exposure, subtle 35mm character, no people, no text, no logo.`

## Retro-futurist seaside resort

- `id`: `retro-futurist-seaside-resort`
- `name_en`: Retro-Futurist Seaside Resort
- `name_zh`: 复古未来海滨度假空间
- `validation_status`: experimental-corpus-reviewed / generation-pending (single-source cluster)
- `trigger`: Fashion editorials, dream hotels, coastal architecture, elegant travel narratives and quiet near-future lifestyle worlds.
- `not_for`: Cyberpunk streets, rustic cabins, crowded family resorts, hard industrial sci-fi.
- `visual_dna`: 1960s resort optimism filtered through a near-future lens; white stone, peach plaster, chrome and glass; arches and sweeping curves; sea horizon as an architectural datum; elegant human scale and intentional emptiness.
- `composition_light_color_material`: Square or 5:4 editorial frame; long axis toward water; wide negative sky or reflective floor; hard clean Mediterranean day, honey sunset or cyan pool night; white, turquoise, coral, pale gold and deep teal.
- `boundaries`: Keep technology implicit in form and finish. Use one or two people at most. Preserve a clear horizon. Avoid spaceships, HUD graphics and generic neon-city signals.
- `template`: `A quiet retro-futurist seaside [HOTEL / RESORT SPACE], 1960s modernist optimism with near-future precision, sweeping white and pale-coral arches, chrome details, glass walls and a clear turquoise sea horizon, one elegant [TRAVELER] used only for scale, generous negative space, [CLEAN DAYLIGHT / HONEY SUNSET / CYAN POOL NIGHT], polished stone reflections, calm premium editorial photography, square or 5:4 frame, no text, no logo.`
- `anti_patterns`: Blade Runner neon; spaceship furniture; crowds; random palm-tree wallpaper; kitschy atomic-age props; oversaturated tropical postcard color.
